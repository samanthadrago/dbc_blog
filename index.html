It's me, Samantha!

Thanks for checking out my blog! I am an aspiring web developer currently enrolled in Dev Bootcamp in San Francisco. After graduating from Harvard in 2011 with a degree in History of Science, I moved to San Francisco to experience life on the West Coast. I worked for a wonderful tech company for almost three years. In my role as Client Services Director, I helped non-profits leverage their volunteers and better serve their communities. But I wanted to challenge myself to learn something new, so I began teaching myself the basics of web development, and decided to take the plunge into a full-time bootcamp.

This blog is comprised of writing inspired by my time in Dev Bootcamp's Phase 0, during which students are encouraged to reflect on what they are learning, both technically and culturally. There are also a handful of one-off web design projects filed under the projects tab. I hope you enjoy checking it out!

When I'm not coding, I spend my time reading in San Francisco's many beautiful parks, riding my bike, hiking, jumping horses over large obstacles, griping about social injustice on Twitter, cooing at puppies, changing my hair color, being snobby about beer, and hanging out with my four backyard chickens.
